The Talmud

The Babylonian Talmud — ~2,700 double-folios of rabbinic legal and narrative material redacted ca. 500 CE. 37 tractates organised into six Sedarim matching the Mishnah. The definitive William Davidson English translation is freely accessible on Sefaria under CC-BY-NC.

Why this page links out instead of rehosting: the William Davidson English Talmud is licensed CC-BY-NC. Rather than mirror ~100 MB of text here, every tractate card below links to the open edition at sefaria.org. Individual daf references (e.g. Sanhedrin 90a) are stable URLs of the form sefaria.org/{Tractate}.{daf}{a|b}.

Citing the Talmud

Talmud references are given as Tractate, page, side — e.g. Sanhedrin 90a means folio 90, side a. Sefaria's URL pattern is sefaria.org/{Tractate}.{N}{a|b}. To jump directly to a specific passage you can append a paragraph number: /Sanhedrin.90a.1.

Catalog entries citing the Talmud link to the specific Sefaria page rather than this index.
